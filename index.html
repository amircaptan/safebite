Allergy Information Guide

Education on your allergies.

Learn how common allergies work, where they appear, and what to watch for when ordering.

  • Nut allergies are one of the most cross-contact-prone allergies in restaurants. Even when a dish doesn’t contain nuts directly, risk often comes from shared equipment rather than ingredients.

    Bakeries, dessert stations, grills, and sauce prep areas frequently use nuts or nut oils, and traces can transfer via chopping boards, blenders, or fryers. Pesto, curries, desserts, dressings, and “chef’s sauces” are common hidden sources.

  • Dairy is widely embedded in restaurant cooking (breads, marinades, mash, soups, sauces, dressings, glazes). Butter is commonly used for finishing without being listed.

    If you have a milk allergy (not just intolerance), treat “creamy”, “rich”, “house sauce” as caution triggers and ask how it’s prepared.

  • Gluten appears in bread, batter, pastry, pasta, soy sauce, gravies, and fried foods due to shared fryers.
  • Shellfish proteins linger in fryers, grills, and oils. Even “plain” chips may be risky if fried together.
  • Soy is common in sauces, marinades, oils, dressings, and vegetarian dishes. Look for soy sauce, tofu, miso, or vegetable protein.
